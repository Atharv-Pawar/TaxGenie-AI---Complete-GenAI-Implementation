SECTION 87A - TAX REBATE
FY 2024-25 | Income Tax Act, 1961

WHAT IS SECTION 87A?
Section 87A provides a rebate (direct reduction in tax payable) for lower-income taxpayers.
It is applied AFTER calculating tax on taxable income but BEFORE adding cess.

REBATE LIMITS FY 2024-25:

OLD TAX REGIME:
- Maximum rebate: ₹12,500
- Eligibility: Net taxable income ≤ ₹5,00,000
- Effect: Zero tax for income up to ₹5,00,000 in Old Regime

NEW TAX REGIME:
- Maximum rebate: ₹25,000
- Eligibility: Net taxable income ≤ ₹7,00,000
- Effect: Zero tax for income up to ₹7,75,000 (₹7L + ₹75K standard deduction)

HOW IT WORKS (Example — New Regime):
Gross Salary:          ₹8,00,000
Less Standard Deduction: ₹75,000
Net Taxable Income:    ₹7,25,000

Tax on ₹7,25,000:
  0-3L:   ₹0
  3-6L:   ₹15,000 (5%)
  6-7.25L: ₹12,500 (10%)
  Total:  ₹27,500

Apply 87A Rebate: ₹27,500 > ₹25,000 limit → Only ₹25,000 rebated
Tax after rebate: ₹2,500
+ 4% cess: ₹100
TOTAL TAX: ₹2,600

COMPARISON FOR ₹7,00,000 NET INCOME (New Regime):
Tax before rebate: ₹25,000 (exactly at limit)
87A rebate: ₹25,000
Tax payable: ₹0 (zero tax!)

ELIGIBILITY:
- Available to RESIDENT individuals only (not NRIs)
- Not available to firms, HUF, companies
- Applied against the basic tax, not surcharge/cess

BUDGET 2025 CHANGES (Effective FY 2025-26):
- New Regime rebate ENHANCED to ₹60,000
- Eligibility threshold raised to ₹12,00,000
- With standard deduction: zero tax up to ₹12,75,000
NOTE: These changes are for FY 2025-26, NOT the current FY 2024-25.

PRACTICAL IMPLICATIONS:
1. If income is just above ₹7L (New Regime), try to reduce it below ₹7L
   through 80CCD(2) employer NPS contribution to get zero tax.
2. For Old Regime: ₹5L threshold means those with ₹5L after all deductions pay zero.
3. The cliff effect: ₹7,00,001 income vs ₹7,00,000 — one extra rupee costs ₹25,000+ in tax.
