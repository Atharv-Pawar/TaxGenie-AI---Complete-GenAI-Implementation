SECTION 80C - DEDUCTIONS FROM TAXABLE INCOME
FY 2024-25 | Income Tax Act, 1961

MAXIMUM DEDUCTION: ₹1,50,000 per financial year

ELIGIBLE INVESTMENTS AND PAYMENTS:

1. EMPLOYEE PROVIDENT FUND (EPF)
   - Employee's own contribution qualifies
   - Employer contribution does NOT qualify under 80C (exempt separately)
   - Minimum 12% of basic salary typically mandated

2. PUBLIC PROVIDENT FUND (PPF)
   - Minimum ₹500/year, Maximum ₹1,50,000/year
   - Lock-in: 15 years (partial withdrawal from 7th year)
   - Returns: 7.1% p.a. (government-set, reviewed quarterly)
   - Returns are FULLY TAX-FREE (EEE status)
   - Loan facility from 3rd to 6th year

3. EQUITY LINKED SAVINGS SCHEME (ELSS)
   - Only mutual fund eligible for 80C
   - Lock-in: 3 years (SHORTEST among 80C instruments)
   - Returns: Market-linked (historically 12-15% CAGR)
   - No upper limit on investment (only ₹1.5L qualifies for deduction)
   - Long-term capital gains above ₹1L taxed at 10%
   - Top performers: Axis ELSS, Mirae Asset Tax Saver, Quant Tax Plan, Parag Parikh

4. LIFE INSURANCE PREMIUM (LIC)
   - Premium for policies on self, spouse, children
   - Premium must be ≤ 10% of sum assured (for policies after April 2012)
   - Maturity proceeds are tax-free if premium < 10% of sum assured

5. NATIONAL SAVINGS CERTIFICATE (NSC)
   - 5-year lock-in
   - Returns: 7.7% p.a. (FY 2024-25)
   - Interest accrued annually also qualifies as reinvestment under 80C
   - Available at Post Offices

6. SUKANYA SAMRIDDHI YOJANA (SSY)
   - For girl children below 10 years
   - Returns: 8.2% p.a. (highest government scheme)
   - Lock-in: Till age 21 of girl child
   - EEE tax status

7. TAX SAVING FIXED DEPOSIT
   - 5-year lock-in at any bank
   - Returns: 6.5-7.5% p.a. (varies by bank)
   - Interest is TAXABLE (only principal qualifies under 80C)

8. HOME LOAN PRINCIPAL REPAYMENT
   - Principal component of EMI qualifies
   - Property must not be sold within 5 years of possession
   - If sold within 5 years, deduction is reversed

9. TUITION FEES
   - For full-time education in India
   - Maximum 2 children per taxpayer
   - Only tuition fees (not development/transport/uniform fees)

10. ULIP (UNIT LINKED INSURANCE PLAN)
    - Premium qualifies under 80C
    - Subject to 10% premium-to-sum-assured ratio

IMPORTANT NOTES:
- The ₹1,50,000 limit is COMBINED across all instruments
- Senior citizens can also invest but may prefer other options
- 80C is available ONLY under Old Tax Regime
- Section 80C + 80CCC + 80CCD(1) combined limit: ₹1,50,000

PLANNING TIP:
For young investors (< 35 years): ELSS is usually the best 80C option
For risk-averse investors: PPF + Tax Saving FD combination
For those needing insurance: LIC Term Plan + ELSS (don't mix insurance + investment)
