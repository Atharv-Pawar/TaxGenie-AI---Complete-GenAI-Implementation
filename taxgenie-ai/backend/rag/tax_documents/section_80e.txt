SECTION 80E - INTEREST ON EDUCATION LOAN
FY 2024-25 | Income Tax Act, 1961

AVAILABLE UNDER: Old Tax Regime ONLY

DEDUCTION LIMIT: NO UPPER LIMIT — full interest paid is deductible

ELIGIBILITY:
- Loan must be taken for higher education
- For self, spouse, children, or student for whom you are legal guardian
- Loan must be from a financial institution or approved charitable institution
- NOT available for loans from friends, relatives, or employers

ELIGIBLE COURSES:
- Any full-time graduate or postgraduate course in science, technology, engineering, mathematics
- Management courses (MBA, PGDM)
- Applied or pure sciences
- Vocational courses (post-secondary)
- Professional courses (medicine, law, engineering)
- Courses pursued in India or abroad

TIME PERIOD:
- Deduction available for the year you start repaying the loan
- Plus the immediately succeeding 7 years
- Maximum benefit period: 8 years

WHAT QUALIFIES:
✅ Interest component of EMI
✅ Pre-payment interest

WHAT DOES NOT QUALIFY:
❌ Principal repayment (not eligible under 80E, may qualify under 80C for housing)
❌ Loans from employer
❌ Personal loans used for education (must be specifically education loan)

DOCUMENTATION:
- Certificate from lender showing interest paid during the year
- Loan sanction letter
- Course admission letter / degree certificate

EXAMPLE:
Education loan outstanding: ₹8,00,000
Interest paid in FY 2024-25: ₹72,000
Deduction under 80E: ₹72,000 (full amount, no limit)
Tax saving at 20% bracket: ₹14,400
Tax saving at 30% bracket: ₹21,600

PLANNING TIP:
If you have an education loan, ensure you're claiming the full interest under 80E.
Many people miss this because they assume there's a limit — there is NONE.
This is especially valuable for foreign education loans which often have high interest.
