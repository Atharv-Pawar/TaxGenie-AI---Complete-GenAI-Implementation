SURCHARGE ON INCOME TAX
FY 2024-25 | Income Tax Act, 1961

WHAT IS SURCHARGE?
Surcharge is an additional tax levied on the income tax of high-income individuals.
It is calculated as a percentage of the income tax (before cess).

SURCHARGE RATES — OLD REGIME (FY 2024-25):
Income Range               | Surcharge Rate
₹50,00,000 - ₹1,00,00,000 | 10%
₹1,00,00,001 - ₹2,00,00,000| 15%
₹2,00,00,001 - ₹5,00,00,000| 25%
Above ₹5,00,00,000         | 37% (reduced to 25% for New Regime)

SURCHARGE RATES — NEW REGIME (FY 2024-25):
Income Range               | Surcharge Rate
₹50,00,000 - ₹1,00,00,000 | 10%
₹1,00,00,001 - ₹2,00,00,000| 15%
₹2,00,00,001 - ₹5,00,00,000| 25%
Above ₹5,00,00,000         | 25% (capped — Budget 2023 relief)

KEY CHANGE (Budget 2023):
Maximum surcharge reduced from 37% to 25% in New Regime.
This makes New Regime significantly more attractive for income > ₹5 crore.

MARGINAL RELIEF:
When income exceeds a surcharge threshold, the additional tax
(surcharge) cannot exceed the additional income. This prevents a
situation where crossing a threshold causes a disproportionate tax.

CALCULATION EXAMPLE (Income ₹60,00,000 — Old Regime):
Tax on ₹60,00,000: approx ₹17,22,500
Surcharge (10%): ₹1,72,250
Total before cess: ₹18,94,750
Health & Education Cess (4%): ₹75,790
TOTAL TAX: ₹19,70,540

NOTE: For most salaried employees earning under ₹50 lakh,
surcharge does NOT apply. Only affects high earners.

EFFECTIVE TAX RATES WITH SURCHARGE + CESS:
- Income ₹50L-₹1Cr: Effective rate up to ~34.32% (Old) / ~34.32% (New)
- Income ₹1-2Cr: Up to ~35.88% (Old) / ~35.88% (New)
- Income > ₹5Cr: Up to ~42.74% (Old) / ~39% (New) — New Regime wins here
