NEW TAX REGIME - FY 2024-25 (AY 2025-26)
Finance Act 2023 | Income Tax Act Section 115BAC

DEFAULT REGIME: New Regime is now the DEFAULT from FY 2023-24 onwards.
Taxpayers must ACTIVELY opt for Old Regime if they want deductions.

TAX SLABS (New Regime FY 2024-25):
Income Range          | Tax Rate
Up to ₹3,00,000       | 0%
₹3,00,001 - ₹6,00,000 | 5%
₹6,00,001 - ₹9,00,000 | 10%
₹9,00,001 - ₹12,00,000| 15%
₹12,00,001 - ₹15,00,000| 20%
Above ₹15,00,000      | 30%

STANDARD DEDUCTION (Budget 2024 Update):
₹75,000 (increased from ₹50,000 in FY 2023-24)

SECTION 87A REBATE (New Regime):
- Rebate up to ₹25,000
- Applicable if taxable income (after standard deduction) ≤ ₹7,00,000
- Makes effective tax ZERO for income up to ₹7.75L (₹7L + ₹75K standard deduction)

HEALTH AND EDUCATION CESS: 4% on tax payable

EFFECTIVE TAX CALCULATION EXAMPLE (New Regime):
Gross Salary: ₹12,00,000
Standard Deduction: ₹75,000
Taxable Income: ₹11,25,000

Tax Calculation:
0-3L: ₹0
3-6L: ₹15,000 (5%)
6-9L: ₹30,000 (10%)
9-11.25L: ₹33,750 (15%)
Total Tax: ₹78,750
+ 4% Cess: ₹3,150
Total: ₹81,900

DEDUCTIONS AVAILABLE IN NEW REGIME:
✅ Standard Deduction: ₹75,000
✅ Employer NPS contribution (Section 80CCD(2)): Up to 10% of salary
✅ Gratuity exemption
✅ Leave encashment exemption
✅ VRS exemption up to ₹5L
✅ Interest on home loan for let-out property (Section 24)
✅ Agriculture income exemption

DEDUCTIONS NOT AVAILABLE IN NEW REGIME:
❌ Section 80C (ELSS, PPF, LIC, PF contribution beyond statutory amount)
❌ Section 80D (Health insurance premium)
❌ HRA Exemption (Section 10(13A))
❌ LTA Exemption
❌ Section 24(b) for self-occupied property (home loan interest)
❌ Section 80E (Education loan interest)
❌ Section 80G (Donations)
❌ Section 80TTA (Savings account interest)
❌ Section 80CCD(1B) (Personal NPS)

WHO SHOULD CHOOSE NEW REGIME:
1. Income ≤ ₹7.75L (zero tax after rebate)
2. Those with deductions below the breakeven point
3. Young earners just starting with minimal investments
4. Those who haven't invested in 80C instruments
5. Those who prefer simplicity over tax planning

WHO SHOULD CHOOSE OLD REGIME:
1. Those with total deductions > ₹3.75L-4.25L (breakeven)
2. Home loan borrowers (80C principal + 24b interest)
3. Those paying high rent with HRA benefit
4. High insurance premium payers

SWITCHING REGIME:
- Salaried: Can switch every year
- Business income taxpayers: Can switch only once from New to Old

FORM TO SUBMIT:
Salaried employees must inform employer at start of year.
Form 10-IEA to be filed for Old Regime declaration.

BUDGET 2025 UPDATE (Proposed):
No changes to New Regime slabs in Budget 2025.
The ₹75,000 standard deduction remains.
