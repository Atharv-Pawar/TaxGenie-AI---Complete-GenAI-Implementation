NPS - NATIONAL PENSION SYSTEM
Section 80CCD | FY 2024-25

OVERVIEW:
NPS is a government-sponsored pension scheme available to all Indian citizens aged 18-70.
It offers market-linked returns with tax benefits — making it one of the best tax-saving tools.

TAX BENEFITS:

1. Section 80CCD(1) — Employee/Self Contribution:
   - Deduction up to 10% of salary (basic + DA) for salaried
   - Up to 20% of gross income for self-employed
   - Included WITHIN the ₹1,50,000 80C limit

2. Section 80CCD(1B) — Additional Contribution (THE KEY BENEFIT):
   - Additional deduction of up to ₹50,000
   - OVER AND ABOVE the ₹1,50,000 limit of 80C
   - Exclusive to NPS (no other instrument gives this)
   - Available ONLY in Old Tax Regime
   - Net tax saving: ₹10,400 at 20% bracket, ₹15,600 at 30% bracket

3. Section 80CCD(2) — Employer Contribution:
   - Deduction for employer's NPS contribution (up to 10% of salary)
   - Available in BOTH Old and New Tax Regime
   - Not part of employee's salary (no TDS)

COMBINED MAXIMUM BENEFIT (Old Regime):
80C (₹1,50,000) + 80CCD(1B) (₹50,000) = ₹2,00,000 in deductions

NPS ACCOUNT TYPES:

Tier-1 Account (for tax benefits):
- Mandatory for NPS subscribers
- Lock-in till age 60 (partial withdrawals allowed after 3 years)
- Can withdraw 60% as lump sum at maturity (tax-free)
- 40% must be used to buy annuity

Tier-2 Account (no lock-in, no tax benefit):
- Optional savings account
- Freely withdrawable
- No tax deduction available (except for government employees)

INVESTMENT OPTIONS:
Auto Choice (Lifecycle Fund): Automatically shifts from equity to debt as you age
Active Choice: You decide allocation across E (equity), C (corporate bonds), G (government securities)

For age < 50: Up to 75% in equity (E) allowed
For age > 50: Equity allocation reduces annually

ANNUITY TAX:
The 40% annuity at maturity is TAXABLE as income in the year received.
Plan annuity income carefully to avoid higher tax bracket at retirement.

PARTIAL WITHDRAWAL RULES:
- After 3 years of subscription
- Maximum 25% of own contribution
- Allowed for specific purposes: education, marriage, illness, house purchase
- Maximum 3 partial withdrawals in lifetime

HOW TO OPEN:
Online: enps.nsdl.com (NSDL eGov) or Karvy
Banks: HDFC, ICICI, SBI, Axis — all offer NPS through their branches
PRAN: Permanent Retirement Account Number assigned upon registration

TOP NPS FUND MANAGERS (by returns):
- HDFC Pension Fund — consistent performer
- ICICI Prudential Pension Fund — aggressive equity
- SBI Pension Fund — conservative, government-heavy
- Kotak Pension Fund — balanced approach

PLANNING TIP:
If you've already maxed 80C (₹1.5L) and are in the 20% or 30% bracket,
NPS 80CCD(1B) is the most powerful additional deduction available.
₹50,000 in NPS saves ₹10,000-₹15,600 in tax depending on your bracket.
