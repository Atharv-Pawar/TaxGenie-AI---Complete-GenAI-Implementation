HRA EXEMPTION - SECTION 10(13A)
FY 2024-25 | Income Tax Act, 1961

AVAILABLE UNDER: Old Tax Regime ONLY

WHAT IS HRA?
House Rent Allowance is part of salary package. The exemption reduces your taxable income.

HRA EXEMPTION CALCULATION - MINIMUM OF THREE:
1. Actual HRA received from employer
2. 50% of basic salary (metro cities) OR 40% of basic salary (non-metro)
3. Actual rent paid MINUS 10% of basic salary

METRO CITIES (50% rule): Delhi, Mumbai, Chennai, Kolkata
NON-METRO (40% rule): All other cities including Bangalore, Hyderabad, Pune, Ahmedabad

EXAMPLE CALCULATION (Mumbai):
- Gross Salary: ₹12,00,000
- Basic Salary: ₹6,00,000
- HRA Received: ₹2,40,000
- Rent Paid: ₹2,40,000/year (₹20,000/month)

Method 1: Actual HRA = ₹2,40,000
Method 2: 50% of Basic = ₹3,00,000
Method 3: Rent - 10% Basic = ₹2,40,000 - ₹60,000 = ₹1,80,000

HRA Exemption = Min(₹2,40,000, ₹3,00,000, ₹1,80,000) = ₹1,80,000
Taxable HRA = ₹2,40,000 - ₹1,80,000 = ₹60,000

ELIGIBILITY CONDITIONS:
1. Employee must receive HRA as part of salary
2. Employee must actually pay rent
3. Employee must not own the property they're living in
4. Rent cannot be paid to spouse

DOCUMENTATION:
- Rent receipts (monthly) with revenue stamp if > ₹5,000/month
- Rent agreement (preferred)
- Landlord's PAN mandatory if annual rent > ₹1,00,000
- Declaration form from employer

CLAIMING HRA:
Option 1: Submit to employer - HRA excluded from TDS
Option 2: Claim in ITR - Fill Schedule HRA in ITR-1

LIVING WITH PARENTS:
- You CAN pay rent to parents
- Parents declare rental income in their ITR
- Rental income likely zero/low for them if house is in their name
- Ensure proper rent receipts and bank transfer

SELF-EMPLOYED:
- Self-employed cannot claim HRA under Section 10(13A)
- Can claim rent under Section 80GG instead (max ₹60,000/year)

COMMON MISTAKES:
1. Not submitting rent receipts to employer → paying excess TDS
2. Annual rent > ₹1L without landlord PAN → claim rejected
3. Paying rent in cash → harder to prove for IT scrutiny
4. Not including HRA while filing ITR

PLANNING TIP:
If you don't receive HRA but pay rent, use Section 80GG (max ₹60,000/year).
If you receive HRA but live in own house, entire HRA is taxable.
