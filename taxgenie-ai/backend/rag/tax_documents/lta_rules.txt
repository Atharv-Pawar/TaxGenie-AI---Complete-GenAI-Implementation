LTA - LEAVE TRAVEL ALLOWANCE EXEMPTION
Section 10(5) | FY 2024-25

AVAILABLE UNDER: Old Tax Regime ONLY

WHAT IS LTA?
Leave Travel Allowance is a component of salary for travel during leave.
The exemption allows you to exclude actual travel expenses from taxable income.

EXEMPTION LIMIT:
- Amount exempt = Actual travel cost incurred (subject to LTA received)
- No fixed rupee cap — capped at actual cost or LTA received, whichever is lower
- Only domestic travel qualifies (international travel does NOT)

FREQUENCY:
- 2 journeys in a block of 4 calendar years
- Current block: 2022-2025
- Next block: 2026-2029
- Unused claims can be carried forward to the first year of the next block

ELIGIBLE TRAVEL:
- Air travel: Economy class airfare of national carrier (Air India) by shortest route
- Rail travel: AC First Class fare by shortest route
- Road travel (no rail/air available): First class or deluxe bus fare

WHO CAN TRAVEL:
- The employee
- Spouse
- Children (up to 2 — children born after October 1, 1998)
- Dependent parents, brothers, sisters

DOCUMENTATION REQUIRED:
- Travel tickets (boarding passes for air)
- Hotel bills are NOT covered (only transport)
- Declaration form to employer

BLOCK CARRY FORWARD RULE:
If you didn't use both LTA claims in 2022-2025 block, you can carry forward ONE claim to use in the first year of the 2026-2029 block. However, you must use it in the calendar year 2026.

WHAT DOESN'T QUALIFY:
❌ International travel
❌ Hotel/accommodation expenses
❌ Food expenses
❌ Local transport at destination
❌ More than 2 trips per block (third trip onwards is fully taxable)

TAX CALCULATION:
LTA received: ₹50,000
Actual travel cost claimed: ₹35,000
Exempt under LTA: ₹35,000 (actual cost)
Taxable LTA: ₹15,000 (balance)

PLANNING TIP:
Plan at least 2 domestic trips every 4 years and submit receipts to HR.
Air travel within India can use up LTA quickly and get full exemption.
