SECTION 80D - DEDUCTION FOR HEALTH INSURANCE PREMIUM
FY 2024-25 | Income Tax Act, 1961

AVAILABLE ONLY UNDER: Old Tax Regime

DEDUCTION LIMITS:

SELF, SPOUSE AND DEPENDENT CHILDREN:
- Maximum: ₹25,000 per year
- If taxpayer is a senior citizen (60+ years): ₹50,000

PARENTS:
- Maximum: ₹25,000 per year
- If parents are senior citizens (60+ years): ₹50,000

MAXIMUM COMBINED DEDUCTION:
- Non-senior taxpayer + Non-senior parents: ₹50,000
- Non-senior taxpayer + Senior citizen parents: ₹75,000
- Senior citizen taxpayer + Senior citizen parents: ₹1,00,000

PREVENTIVE HEALTH CHECK-UP:
- Included within the above limits
- Maximum: ₹5,000
- Can be paid in CASH (unlike insurance premium)
- For self, spouse, children, OR parents

ELIGIBLE PREMIUMS:
1. Medical insurance premium (health insurance)
2. Contribution to Central Government Health Scheme (CGHS)
3. Notified health scheme contributions
4. Preventive health check-up (cash payment allowed)

NOT ELIGIBLE:
- Premiums paid in cash (except preventive check-up)
- Group insurance premium paid by employer (employer's deduction, not yours)
- Premiums for policies where claim is made in same year

PAYMENT MODE:
- Premium must be paid by any mode OTHER than cash
- Cheque, NEFT, credit card, UPI all acceptable
- Cash payment ONLY for preventive health check-up (up to ₹5,000)

- HDFC Ergo Optima Secure

- Care Supreme

Senior Citizen Plans (Parents):
- Star Health Senior Citizen Red Carpet

DOCUMENTATION REQUIRED:
- Premium receipt from insurance company
- Policy document
- For preventive check-up: receipts from diagnostic center

PRACTICAL TIPS:
1. Buy health insurance even if your employer provides group cover
2. ₹5L sum insured minimum recommended; ₹10L+ for metros
3. Include parents in separate policy for additional 80D benefit
4. Annual premium payment qualifies for full year deduction
5. Top-up plans are also eligible for 80D deduction
