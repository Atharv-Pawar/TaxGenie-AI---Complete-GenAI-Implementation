SECTION 80TTA / 80TTB - SAVINGS ACCOUNT INTEREST DEDUCTION
FY 2024-25 | Income Tax Act, 1961

AVAILABLE UNDER: Old Tax Regime ONLY

SECTION 80TTA (For non-senior citizens — below 60 years):
- Maximum deduction: ₹10,000 per year
- Eligible interest: Savings account interest from banks, co-ops, post offices
- NOT applicable to: Fixed deposits, recurring deposits, time deposits

SECTION 80TTB (For senior citizens — 60 years and above):
- Maximum deduction: ₹50,000 per year
- Eligible interest: ALL bank interest including FD, RD, savings accounts
- Replaces 80TTA for senior citizens (use one or the other, not both)

HOW SAVINGS INTEREST IS TAXED:
1. Bank credits interest to your account
2. Interest is added to your "Income from Other Sources"
3. Deduction under 80TTA/80TTB reduces taxable income
4. Net taxable interest = Interest earned - 80TTA/80TTB deduction

EXAMPLE (Non-senior citizen):
Savings account interest earned: ₹15,000
80TTA deduction: ₹10,000
Taxable interest: ₹5,000

DOCUMENTS:
- Bank statement / passbook showing interest credited
- Form 26AS should reflect TDS if any

PLANNING TIP:
This is a small but easy deduction that many people miss.
Ensure you include savings interest in your ITR under "Other Sources"
and claim the 80TTA deduction simultaneously.
If interest > ₹10,000, only the excess is taxable.

NOTE: This is available only in Old Regime.
New Regime taxpayers cannot claim 80TTA/80TTB deduction.
